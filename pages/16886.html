Sunroof Drain Inspection and Cleaning (Fixed Roof Window)

Inspection and Cleaning Procedure

Ultra View roofs have 1 drain, located under the roof side trim covers, in each rear body pillar.

1. Lower the rear headliner. Refer to Headlining Trim Panel Replacement (Service and Repair) .
2. Remove the rear stationary window. Refer to Sunroof Window Replacement (Front) (Service and Repair)Sunroof Window Replacement (Rear Vent) (Service and Repair)Sunroof Window Replacement (Center) (Service and Repair)Sunroof Window Replacement (Rear Stationary) (Service and Repair) .
3. Pour a small amount of water into the sunroof module drain trough. Inspect for a plugged or disconnected drainage system. If the water flow is restricted, perform the following steps:

Caution: Refer to Safety Glasses and Compressed Air Caution (Safety Glasses and Compressed Air Warning) .

Important: Use care when using compressed air to clear the sunroof drain hose. Do not exceed 241 kPa (35 psi).


* Use a flexible wire or 241 kPa (35 psi) of compressed air to clear any debris plugging the drain system.
* Inspect the drainage system for proper water drainage.
* If water drainage is still not adequate, remove the headliner. Inspect the sunroof drain hoses for proper routing. Refer to Headlining Trim Panel Replacement (Service and Repair) .

4. Install the rear stationary window. Refer to Sunroof Window Replacement (Front) (Service and Repair)Sunroof Window Replacement (Rear Vent) (Service and Repair)Sunroof Window Replacement (Center) (Service and Repair)Sunroof Window Replacement (Rear Stationary) (Service and Repair) .
5. Install the rear headliner. Refer to Headlining Trim Panel Replacement (Service and Repair) .
