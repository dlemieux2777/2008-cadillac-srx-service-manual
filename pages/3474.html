Front Suspension



Stabilizer Shaft Replacement

Removal Procedure


Notice: The ball stud must not rotate during disassembly or reassembly. Hand tools must be used to keep the ball stud from rotating. If air tools are used and the stud is allowed to rotate, damage to the ball stud and/or stud mounting hole may occur.

1. Raise and support the vehicle. Refer to Lifting and Jacking the Vehicle (Service and Repair) .
2. Remove the tire and wheel. Refer to Tire and Wheel Removal and Installation (Service and Repair) .




3. Remove the stabilizer shaft link to stabilizer shaft retaining nuts. Refer to Stabilizer Shaft Link Replacement (Front Suspension) .
4. Disconnect the stabilizer shaft links from the stabilizer shaft.
5. Remove the stabilizer shaft mounting bolts and brackets.
6. Remove the stabilizer shaft insulators from the stabilizer shaft.
7. Remove the stabilizer shaft from the vehicle.



Installation Procedure

1. Install the stabilizer shaft to the vehicle.
2. Install the stabilizer shaft insulators to the stabilizer shaft. Install the insulator to the stabilizer shaft with the slit facing rearward.

Important: Do not tighten the bolts at this time.


3. Install the stabilizer shaft brackets and mounting bolts.




4. Connect the stabilizer shaft links to the stabilizer shaft. Refer to Stabilizer Shaft Link Replacement (Front Suspension) .

Notice: Refer to Fastener Notice (Fastener Notice) .

5. Install the stabilizer shaft link retaining nuts.

* Tighten the link nuts to 115 N.m (95 lb ft).
* Tighten the bracket bolts to 110 N.m (81 lb ft).


6. Install the tire and wheel. Refer to Tire and Wheel Removal and Installation (Service and Repair) .
7. Lower the vehicle.
