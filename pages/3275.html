Heated Steering Wheel Indicator Malfunction



Heated Steering Wheel Indicator Malfunction

Diagnostic Instructions

* Perform the Diagnostic System Check - Vehicle (Initial Inspection and Diagnostic Overview) prior to using this diagnostic procedure.
* Strategy Based Diagnosis (Initial Inspection and Diagnostic Overview)
* Diagnostic Procedure Instructions (Initial Inspection and Diagnostic Overview)



Circuit/System Description

When the ignition is ON and the heated steering wheel switch is pressed, the switch circuit is pulled to ground. The heat module monitors the switch circuit, and turns the steering wheel heat system ON or OFF when the circuit is grounded. The heat module supplies voltage to the indicator lamp when the system is on.

Circuit/System Verification

1. Ignition ON, press and release the heated steering wheel switch, located on the left side of the steering wheel. Observe that the heated steering wheel indicator illuminates.
2. Press and release the heated steering wheel switch again. Observe that the indicator turns OFF.



Circuit/System Testing

1. Ignition OFF, disable the SIR system and remove the inflatable restraint module from the steering wheel.
2. Remove and disconnect the heat control module, located at the top of the steering wheel.
3. Ignition ON, measure resistance between ground circuit terminal B and ground.

• If greater than 1.0 ohm, repair the open or high resistance in the ground circuit.

4. Test for battery voltage on the ignition 1 voltage circuit terminal A of the heat control module connector.

• If battery voltage is not present, test for and repair the open in the ignition 1 voltage circuit.

5. Measure voltage between the ignition voltage circuit terminal A and the switch circuit terminal B. Press and release the heated steering wheel switch. Verify that there is battery voltage between terminals A and B when the switch is pressed, and no voltage when the switch is released.

• If there is no voltage when the switch is pressed, test the switch circuit for an open. If the circuit tests normal, test or replace the switch.

6. Connect a 3 A fused jumper between the indicator control circuit terminal 2 and the ignition voltage circuit terminal A. Verify that the heated wheel indicator illuminates when terminals 2 and A are jumpered together.

• If the indicator does not illuminate when terminals 2 and A are jumpered, test the indicator control circuit for an open, short to ground, or high resistance. If the circuit tests normal, test or replace the switch.

7. If all circuits test normal, replace the steering wheel heat control module.
