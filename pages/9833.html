Secondary Camshaft Drive Chain Replacement - Left



Secondary Camshaft Drive Chain Replacement - Left Side


Tools Required
* J 39946 Crankshaft Socket
* EN 46328 Camshaft Holding Tool



Removal Procedure

1. Remove the right secondary timing chain. Refer to Secondary Camshaft Drive Chain Replacement - Right Side (Secondary Camshaft Drive Chain Replacement - Right) .




2. Using the J 39946 , rotate the crankshaft until the primary timing gear alignment marks (1) are adjacent to each other as shown.
3. Remove the left camshaft position actuator housing. DO NOT remove the actuator solenoids from the housing. Refer to Camshaft Position Actuator Housing Replacement - Left Side (Camshaft Position Actuator Housing Replacement - Left Side) .




Caution: Refer to Camshaft Holding Tool Caution (Camshaft Holding Tool Caution) .
4. Install the EN 46328 (1) on the bank 2 (left) camshafts (2).




5. Loosen and remove the left secondary timing chain tensioner bolts and tensioner. Refer to Secondary Camshaft Drive Chain Tensioner Replacement - Left Side (Secondary Camshaft Drive Chain Tensioner Replacement - Left) .





Notice: Refer to Torque Reaction Against Timing Drive Chain Notice (Torque Reaction Against Timing Drive Chain Notice) .

6. Use an open-end wrench on the hex cast into the camshaft in order to prevent the camshaft from rotating when removing the camshaft oil control valve.
7. Loosen and remove the bank 2 (left) exhaust camshaft position oil control valve.
8. Slide the left exhaust camshaft position actuator off of the camshaft and remove the secondary timing chain from the camshaft actuator teeth.





Notice: Refer to Torque Reaction Against Timing Drive Chain Notice (Torque Reaction Against Timing Drive Chain Notice) .

9. Use an open-end wrench on the hex cast into the camshaft in order to prevent the camshaft from rotating when removing the camshaft oil control valve.
10. Loosen and remove the bank 2 (left) intake camshaft position oil control valve.
11. Slide the left intake camshaft position actuator off of the camshaft and remove the secondary timing chain from the camshaft actuator teeth.
12. Remove the left secondary timing chain from the engine.
13. Clean and inspect the camshaft timing drive components. Refer to Camshaft Timing Drive Components Cleaning and Inspection () .



Installation Procedure





1. The secondary timing chain (2) has 3 black links that aid in timing the camshaft position actuators to the intermediate sprocket.
2. The black link (4) is aligned with the bank 2 exhaust actuator timing mark. The black link (3) is aligned with the bank 2 intake actuator timing mark.
3. The black link (1) is aligned with the intermediate sprocket.




4. The intermediate sprocket left bank timing mark is labeled left bank (LB) as shown.
5. Assemble the secondary timing chain to the intermediate sprocket aligning the sprocket LB timing mark to the timing chain black link.




6. Align the timing mark (5) of the LB intake camshaft position actuator with the timing chain black link (3) and install the actuator on the camshaft with the actuator timing mark perpendicular (90 degrees) to the cylinder head deck surface at the top of its rotation.
7. Loosely install the oil control valve (10) to secure the intake actuator.





Notice: Refer to Torque Reaction Against Timing Drive Chain Notice (Torque Reaction Against Timing Drive Chain Notice) .


Notice: Refer to Fastener Notice (Fastener Notice) .

8. Use an open-end wrench on the hex cast into the camshaft in order to prevent the camshaft from rotating when tightening the oil control valve.

Tighten the oil control valve to 120 N.m (89 lb ft).





9. Align the timing mark (7) of the LB exhaust camshaft position actuator with the timing chain black link (4) and install the actuator on the camshaft with the actuator timing mark perpendicular (90 degrees) to the cylinder head deck surface at the top of its rotation.
10. Loosely install the oil control valve (8) to secure the exhaust actuator.





Notice: Refer to Torque Reaction Against Timing Drive Chain Notice (Torque Reaction Against Timing Drive Chain Notice) .

11. Use an open-end wrench on the hex cast into the camshaft in order to prevent the camshaft from rotating when tightening the oil control valve.

Tighten the oil control valve to 120 N.m (89 lb ft).





12. Install the left secondary timing chain tensioner. Refer to Secondary Camshaft Drive Chain Tensioner Replacement - Left Side (Secondary Camshaft Drive Chain Tensioner Replacement - Left) .
13. Install the right secondary timing chain. Refer to Secondary Camshaft Drive Chain Replacement - Right Side (Secondary Camshaft Drive Chain Replacement - Right) .
14. Remove the EN 46328 .
15. Install the left camshaft position actuator housing. Refer to Camshaft Position Actuator Housing Replacement - Left Side (Camshaft Position Actuator Housing Replacement - Left Side) .
